All motorcycle categories at a glance

It doesn't matter whether it's a motorcycle or a scooter: In order to be able to take the test, you need the practical basic training for motorcycles (PGS). The basic course is compulsory and you must have completed it within 4 months of the date your learner driver’s license was issued. The basic courses basically consist of 3 course parts (part 1-3) of 4 hours each (total 12 hours). Whether you have to attend the basic course depends on your previous qualifications. Your learner driver’s license usually states how many hours you have to complete.

12 hour basic course (basic course part 1-3)

  • Category AM (up to 50ccm, from 15 years)
  • Category A1 (up to 125ccm, from 16 years)
  • Category A2 (A limited, over 11kW, max 35kW, from 18 years of age) without category A1 ID

4 hours basic course (part 3)

Anyone who limits category A or wants to achieve A, already owns category A1 and attended the basic course before January 1st, 2021, only has to catch up on part 3. Persons who received the category A or A- learner’s license before January 1st, 2021 only have to complete the 4-hour basic course (even if the license says 6 hours).

No basic course

The practical basic training (PGS) is valid indefinitely after completion. All persons who attended the 12-hour basic course after January 1st, 2021 are therefore exempt from the basic course.

Please note: The individual parts of the course may not be completed on the same day and the order of the parts of the course must be observed. It should also be noted that the vehicle for the basic course must correspond to the category according to the learner's license.

Depending on your age and which tickets you already have, different conditions apply. You can get an overview here . We have summarized the most important things for you in a few steps (valid from 2021).

If you have a driver's license

If you already have a valid driver's license, you are only a few steps away from the driver's license:

  • Apply for a learner's license for a motorcycle (with an eye test if it was more than 2 years ago)
  • Obtain a learner's license from the road traffic office
  • Attend the three basic motorcycle courses within four months
  • Pass the practical test (only cat. A and A limited).
  • Drive accident-free for two years and obtain a definitive driver's license

If you don't have a driver's license

  • Complete an emergency course
  • Apply for a learner's license for a motorcycle (with an eye test)
  • Pass the theory test and receive a learner's license
  • Attend the three basic motorcycle courses within four months.
  • Attend traffic education lessons (VKU).
  • Pass the practical exam.
  • Drive accident-free for two years and obtain a definitive driver's license
  • WAB course (only cat. A and A limited)
